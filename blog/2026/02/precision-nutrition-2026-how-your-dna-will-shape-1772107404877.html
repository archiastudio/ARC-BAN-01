1. The Science Behind Nutrigenomics

Nutrigenomics studies the interaction between nutrients and genes. By analyzing single nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs), scientists can predict how your body processes fats, carbohydrates, vitamins, and minerals.

2. Key Genetic Markers for Personalized Diets

  • FTO Gene: Influences appetite and fat storage.
  • APOE Gene: Affects cholesterol metabolism.
  • TCF7L2 Gene: Linked to insulin response and blood sugar control.
  • MTHFR Gene: Determines folate metabolism and cardiovascular risk.
  • PPARG Gene: Regulates fat cell differentiation and insulin sensitivity.
